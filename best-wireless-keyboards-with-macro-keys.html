Best Wireless Keyboards with Programmable Macro Keys for Video Editing

By Karim • May 18, 2026

For professional video editors, streamers, and graphic designers, a standard office keyboard is a massive bottleneck. When you are scrubbing through heavy 4K timelines in Adobe Premiere, DaVinci Resolve, or Final Cut Pro, every second spent moving your mouse to click a software tool adds up to hours of lost productivity. Creative professionals require dedicated hardware—keyboards outfitted with programmable macro columns, custom modifier layers, and tactile rotary knobs designed specifically for timeline scrubbing. By mapping complex multi-key cuts and color-grading macros to a single physical press, you transform your desk into an editing command center. We reviewed the absolute best macro-heavy wireless keyboards engineered to accelerate your creative workflow.

1. The Dedicated Macro King: Logitech G915 LIGHTSPEED

The Logitech G915 LIGHTSPEED remains the absolute gold standard for creative professionals who need immediate access to complex shortcuts. Its defining feature is the dedicated vertical column of five 'G-keys' located on the far left edge of the chassis. Using the Logitech G HUB software, editors can program these keys with custom multi-step macros or app-specific profiles for Premiere Pro and After Effects. Built from aircraft-grade aluminum alloy, it features ultra-fast low-profile mechanical switches and a precision-engineered edgeless metal volume wheel that serves as an exceptional tool for scrubbing media or adjusting audio levels on the fly.

2. The Firmware Mastermind: Keychron Q1 Max

While the Keychron Q1 Max looks like a standard premium 75% mechanical keyboard, its internal firmware makes it an editing powerhouse. Driven by a powerful ARM Cortex-M4 chip and full open-source QMK/VIA compatibility, editors can remap literally every single key on the board across multiple customized layers. The standout hardware feature is the programmable CNC-machined metal knob in the top right corner, which is flawlessly designed for scrubbing timelines or fine-tuning brush sizes in Photoshop. The entire body is crafted from 6063 solid aluminum, providing a massive, immovable footprint on your desk while you work.

3. The Modular Architecture: ASUS ROG Claymore II

The ASUS ROG Claymore II offers a genuinely revolutionary design for right-handed video editors: a fully detachable modular numpad. Instead of keeping the numpad on the right (which forces your hand away from your mouse), you can snap the numpad directly onto the left side of the keyboard. This turns the numpad into a massive, left-handed macro control deck featuring four dedicated hotkeys and a massive volume wheel, allowing you to trigger complex shortcuts with your left hand while never letting go of your mouse with your right. It operates on a superfast 1ms 2.4GHz RF wireless connection to guarantee zero latency during rapid editing cuts.

4. The Compact Commander: ASUS ROG Strix Scope II

If you want maximum macro capability without the massive footprint of a modular board, the ROG Strix Scope II delivers an incredible 96% layout that is only 1cm wider than a standard TKL keyboard. It retains a full numpad for data entry and timecode typing, while featuring pre-programmed hotkeys and an intuitive multi-function wheel for timeline and media scrubbing. Utilizing ROG SpeedNova 2.4GHz wireless tech, it boasts an exceptional 1,500-hour battery life. Furthermore, every single key is fully macro-programmable through the Armoury Crate software, allowing deep customization for complex streaming or editing setups.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is a rotary knob important for video editing?

A programmable rotary encoder knob allows editors to smoothly scrub forward and backward through their video timeline frame-by-frame, which is significantly faster and more precise than clicking and dragging with a mouse.

Can I program macros on Mac OS?

Yes. Keyboards running QMK/VIA (like Keychron) or those with dedicated Mac software variants allow full macro mapping specifically for Apple ecosystems.
