Best Low-Profile Mechanical Keyboards for Mac and PC

By Karim • May 17, 2026

The chunky, towering mechanical keyboards of the past are being replaced by something much sleeker. Low-profile mechanical keyboards offer the incredibly satisfying tactile feedback of traditional switches, but in a slim, minimalist chassis that rivals Apple's Magic Keyboard. They require less wrist extension and look stunning on a modern desk. Whether you are a Mac loyalist or a Windows power user, we have tested the absolute best low-profile mechanical keyboards that seamlessly bridge the gap between aesthetic design and premium typing performance.

1. The Best for Enthusiasts: NuPhy Air75 V2

NuPhy has taken the mechanical keyboard community by storm, and the Air75 V2 proves exactly why. Wrapped in a premium aluminum frame with a new translucent bottom shell, this 75% layout keyboard is visually stunning. It comes equipped with high-quality PBT keycaps that resist finger oils and shine, ensuring the board looks brand new even after months of heavy use. The dual RGB light bars on the sides cleverly indicate battery life and connection mode.

Where the Air75 V2 truly shines is under the hood. It features a massive 4000mAh battery (yielding up to 220 hours without backlighting) and supports QMK/VIA firmware. This open-source software allows you to limitlessly remap keys and customize macros, making it a dream for tinkerers. With Bluetooth 5.1, a 2.4G dongle, and wired modes, it seamlessly jumps between Mac, Windows, and even mobile devices.

2. The Best for Professionals: Logitech MX Mechanical Mini

If you want the satisfaction of mechanical switches but need a keyboard that blends seamlessly into a high-end corporate office, the Logitech MX Mechanical Mini is the undisputed champion. It features a sleek, dual-tone metal build and low-profile clicky switches that provide highly gratifying, audible tactile feedback without the extreme height of traditional gaming keyboards.

Logitech’s software ecosystem is its greatest strength. The smart illumination automatically lights up the moment your hands approach the keys and adjusts to your room's lighting. Combined with the Logi Options+ app, you can easily customize function keys or use Logitech Flow to seamlessly copy and paste text across multiple computers simultaneously. It connects flawlessly via Bluetooth or the included Logi Bolt receiver.

Who Should Buy Which?

Buy the NuPhy Air75 V2 if:

  • You love to customize your hardware (QMK/VIA).
  • You prefer the durability of PBT keycaps.
  • You want a fun, modern aesthetic with long battery life.

Buy the Logitech MX Mechanical Mini if:

  • You work in a corporate setting.
  • You rely heavily on Logitech's software ecosystem (Logi Options+).
  • You want proximity-sensing backlighting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will these work seamlessly with Mac?

Yes, both keyboards are fully compatible with macOS. The NuPhy even comes with Mac-specific modifier keycaps (Command/Option) in the box, and the Logitech automatically maps to Mac layouts when connected to an Apple device.

What is the difference between PBT and ABS keycaps?

PBT (used on the NuPhy) is a denser, higher-quality plastic that has a slightly textured feel and resists developing a greasy shine over time. ABS (used on the Logitech) is smoother and more common, but tends to show finger oils much faster.
