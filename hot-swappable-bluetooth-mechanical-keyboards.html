Best Hot-Swappable Bluetooth Mechanical Keyboards

By Karim • May 18, 2026

The ultimate flex in the mechanical keyboard world isn't just having a great-looking board—it is having a board that sounds and feels exactly how you want it to. Hot-swappable keyboards allow you to pull out individual mechanical switches and replace them with new ones without ever touching a soldering iron. Whether you want a deep, creamy 'thock' for typing or a fast, linear 'clack' for gaming, a hot-swappable PCB is the ultimate canvas for customization. We tested the best wireless hot-swappable boards that deliver enthusiast-grade performance right out of the box.

1. The Best Out-of-the-Box Sound: Epomaker TH80 V2

If you want a keyboard that sounds heavily modded and customized without actually doing any work yourself, the Epomaker TH80 V2 is an absolute powerhouse. It features a 75% layout, keeping your essential function keys while saving desk space, and includes a highly sought-after metal rotary knob for volume control. What truly sets it apart is the acoustic engineering inside the case.

Epomaker packed this board with Poron, IXPE, latex, and silicone dampening layers. When combined with the pre-lubed switches and thick PBT keycaps, every keystroke delivers a deep, satisfying 'thock' free of metallic ping. The massive 8000mAh battery delivers over 200 hours of continuous play, and the tri-mode connectivity seamlessly juggles between your gaming PC, Mac, and tablet.

2. The Best Budget 60% Layout: Royal Kludge RK61

The Royal Kludge RK61 is legendary in the mechanical keyboard community. It is the undisputed king of budget 60% keyboards, beloved by FPS gamers for freeing up massive amounts of mouse space. By eliminating the number pad, arrow keys, and function row, you are left with only the essentials in a beautiful, minimalist block.

The standout feature at this price point is the 5-pin hot-swappable PCB. This makes the RK61 the perfect 'starter board' for the custom keyboard hobby. You can use it out of the box, and later down the line, buy a pack of premium Akko or Gateron switches and easily swap them in. It features stable Bluetooth 5.0, a 2.4Ghz dongle, and USB-C wired modes, making it incredibly versatile for both gaming and portable typing.

  • You want a premium, deep 'thock' sound immediately without modifying anything.
  • You rely on dedicated arrow and function keys.

Buy the Royal Kludge RK61 if:

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 3-pin vs 5-pin hot-swappable mean?

Mechanical switches have either 3 or 5 plastic/metal pins on the bottom. A 5-pin hot-swap board (like the RK61) is universally compatible and will accept almost any switch on the market. A 3-pin board will require you to clip off two of the plastic legs on a 5-pin switch before it fits.

Do I need to solder to change switches?

No! That is the beauty of a hot-swappable board. You simply use a switch puller tool (usually included in the box) to gently pull the switch straight up and out of the socket.
