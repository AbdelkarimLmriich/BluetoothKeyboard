Frequently Asked Questions

Are mechanical switches or scissor switches better for fast data entry?

It comes down to tactile feedback. Scissor switches (like the MX Keys) offer a much shorter travel distance, which can slightly increase WPM for light typists. However, mechanical switches (like the Keychron Q6 Max) provide a highly distinct physical actuation bump, which drastically reduces accidental double-typing and false inputs during rapid number entry.

Do I need a 100% full-size keyboard for accounting, or is a 96% layout enough?

A 96% (or 1800) layout is generally the perfect sweet spot for modern accounting. It retains the exact layout of the 10-key number pad you need for data entry but pushes it flush against the arrow keys, saving several inches of desk space for your mouse and physical ledgers.
