Best Quiet Bluetooth Keyboards for Zoom Calls

By Karim • May 2026

There is nothing worse than being unmuted on a 20-person Zoom call and realizing everyone can hear you aggressively typing an email. Standard mechanical keyboards are notorious for their loud, clacky acoustics. 1. The Best Overall: Logitech MX Keys S

If your primary goal is to be completely undetectable on a microphone, the Logitech MX Keys S is the undisputed champion. During our testing on both Zoom and Microsoft Teams, the spherically-dished scissor switches were practically inaudible to our teammates, even when typing aggressively.

What makes this keyboard a true productivity powerhouse is the Logi Options+ app. You can program "Smart Actions" to automate repetitive tasks—meaning you can map a single key to instantly mute your microphone and turn off your camera simultaneously. It is a lifesaver for remote workers.

2. The Best Mechanical Option: ProtoArc K310 Pro

We know many of our readers refuse to give up mechanical switches. If you need that authentic mechanical feel but want to keep noise levels minimal, the ProtoArc K310 Pro is a hidden gem. Instead of loud, clicky Blue switches, it uses low-profile Red linear switches.

During our office tests, the linear travel provided a smooth keystroke without the sharp "clack" at the actuation point. Because the keys are low-profile, the travel distance is shorter, which further dampens the sound. Wrapped in a sleek aluminum top case, it looks and feels like a keyboard twice its price.

Who Should Buy Which?

Buy the Logitech MX Keys S if:

  • You are frequently on high-stakes video calls and need absolute silence.
  • You want to automate macros and shortcuts for productivity software.
  • You prefer the flat, fast feel of an Apple Magic Keyboard.

Buy the ProtoArc K310 Pro if:

  • You are a mechanical keyboard purist who needs a compromise for the office.
  • You require a full number pad for data entry or coding.
  • You are on a budget but want a premium aluminum build.

Frequently Asked Questions

What switch color is best for Zoom calls?

If you must use a mechanical keyboard, always opt for Red (Linear) or Silent Red switches. They lack the tactile "bump" and the audible click of Brown or Blue switches, making them the quietest option for open offices and microphones.

Can noise-canceling software block mechanical keyboard sounds?

Programs like Krisp or Zoom's built-in "High" background noise suppression are very good at filtering out keyboard clicks. However, aggressive noise cancellation can sometimes clip the start of your own voice. Starting with a quieter keyboard is always the best foundation.
